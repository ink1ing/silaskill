# Component Guidelines

Use these heuristics to make the palette feel coherent in actual interfaces.

## Overall Composition

- Favor quiet layouts with strong spacing discipline over dense dashboards
- Use off-white or warm dark backgrounds across the whole frame, not only inside cards
- Prefer a small number of surface layers
- Keep shadows subtle; separation should come more from tone than heavy blur

## Cards And Panels

- Main cards should feel like paper or lightly lifted sheets
- Secondary containers should use `surfaceVariant`
- Borders can be thin and quiet; avoid heavy outlines on every surface
- Radii should usually sit in the moderate range, not extreme pills everywhere

## Inputs

- Inputs should look integrated into the surface system
- Use `surfaceVariant` for filled inputs or muted composer areas
- Preserve strong text contrast and clearly visible focus states
- Accent the active state with the primary brown-gold color sparingly

## Text

- Headings should feel editorial, not hyper-technical
- Long-form copy benefits from slightly increased line height
- Secondary text should remain readable; do not fade it into the background
- Code blocks should be calm and legible, with consistent foreground and subtle container contrast

## Navigation

- Sidebars and top bars should feel structural, not glossy
- Active state should rely on tone and slight emphasis before color saturation
- Avoid bright icon systems that fight the neutral palette

## Buttons And Links

- Primary actions use the brown-gold accent
- Secondary actions should often be tonal rather than outlined
- Text links can share the same accent token but should not overpower surrounding copy

## What To Avoid

- Pure white backgrounds paired with the Claude-aligned accent color
- Neon accents or highly saturated gradients
- Overly glassy effects, strong borders, or heavy drop shadows
- Literal cloning of Claude layouts, logos, or interaction copy
